Mortgage & Insurance

Understanding how spray foam affects your mortgage eligibility and insurance coverage - and how we can help restore both.

The Growing Problem

Spray foam insulation, once promoted as an energy-saving solution, is now causing serious issues for homeowners trying to sell, remortgage, or insure their properties. Major lenders and insurers are increasingly rejecting properties with spray foam installations.

Mortgage Lending Issues

Why Lenders Are Concerned

  • Structural Inspection: Foam prevents proper assessment of roof timbers and structural elements
  • Moisture Risk: Potential for condensation and water damage behind foam
  • Building Standards: Many installations don't meet current building regulations
  • Future Maintenance: Inability to maintain or repair roof structures
  • Removal Costs: Potential expensive remediation required

Insurance Coverage Issues

Insurance Concerns

Many insurance providers are now excluding or restricting coverage for properties with spray foam insulation due to:

Risk Factors
  • Moisture-related damage
  • Fire safety concerns
  • Pest infestation risks
  • Maintenance difficulties
Common Exclusions
  • Water damage claims
  • Structural damage
  • Roof-related issues
  • Policy non-renewal
